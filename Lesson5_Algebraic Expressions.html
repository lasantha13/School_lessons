The Lesson Map

1. The Power of Substitution (Even with Hard Numbers!)

You've substituted whole numbers before, but now we're leveling up! We'll use **directed numbers**—that means fractions (1/2) and negative numbers (-3).

The Rule:

Simply replace the variable with the given value. Remember your rules for multiplying and adding fractions and negatives!

Complex Concept Deep Dive: Negative Fractions

When you have a negative fraction, like b = -2/3, the negative sign applies to the whole fraction. When you multiply it, remember: **Negative × Positive = Negative**, and **Negative × Negative = Positive**.

Example: Find the value of 5 - 6b when b = -2/3.

1. **Substitute:** 5 - 6 * (-2/3)

2. **Multiply:** -6 * (-2/3). The two negatives cancel, making it positive. 6 * 2 / 3 = 12 / 3 = 4.

3. **Calculate:** 5 + 4 = 9.

2. The FOIL Method: Multiplying Binomials

Multiplying two binomials like (x+3)(x+2) is like opening a combination lock. Every term in the first lock has to pair up with every term in the second lock! We use the **FOIL** method to make sure we don't miss anything.

What is FOIL?
  • **F**irst: Multiply the **First** terms: x * x = x²
  • **O**uter: Multiply the **Outer** terms: x * 2 = 2x
  • **I**nner: Multiply the **Inner** terms: 3 * x = 3x
  • **L**ast: Multiply the **Last** terms: 3 * 2 = 6

Then, add them all up and simplify: x² + 2x + 3x + 6 = x² + 5x + 6.

Detailed Example: Dealing with Negatives

The toughest part is handling the negative signs! Keep track of them carefully.

Example: Expand (x - 5)(x + 3)

F: x * x = x²

O: x * 3 = 3x

I: -5 * x = -5x (Watch the negative!)

L: -5 * 3 = -15 (Negative * Positive = Negative)

Combine: x² + 3x - 5x - 15 = x² - 2x - 15.

3. Geometry Check: Area of a Rectangle

Why does FOIL work? Because algebra is just a way to talk about shapes! The product of two binomials is exactly the same as finding the **Area** of a larger rectangle.

The Area Model for (x + 3)(x + 2)

Imagine a rectangle with:
Length = (x + 3) and Breadth = (x + 2).

The total area is divided into 4 smaller areas:

  • **1st Area (Square):** x * x = x²
  • **2nd Area (Rectangle):** x * 2 = 2x
  • **3rd Area (Rectangle):** 3 * x = 3x
  • **4th Area (Small Rectangle):** 3 * 2 = 6

Total Area = x² + 2x + 3x + 6 = x² + 5x + 6. It matches the FOIL result! **Algebra = Geometry!**
