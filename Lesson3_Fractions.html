Lesson 0: Quick Fraction Fundamentals Review

Before we dive into the advanced topics, let's quickly review the essential fraction concepts you've mastered!

0.1 Fraction Types & Conversion

  • **Proper Fraction:** Numerator is smaller than the denominator (e.g., 3/5).
  • **Improper Fraction:** Numerator is equal to or larger than the denominator (e.g., 5/3).
  • **Mixed Number:** A whole number and a proper fraction combined (e.g., 1 2/3).
  • **Conversion Key:** To convert a mixed number (1 2/3) to an improper fraction: (1 × 3) + 2 = 5. Result: 5/3.

0.2 Equivalent Fractions

You can get an equivalent fraction by multiplying (or dividing) the numerator and denominator by the **same number** (that isn't zero).

Example: 2/5 = (2 × 2) / (5 × 2) = **4/10**

0.3 Reciprocals and Division

Dividing by a fraction is the same as multiplying by its **reciprocal** (the fraction flipped upside down).

Example: 4/3 $\div$ 2 is the same as 4/3 × **1/2**

Lesson 1: The Secret Power of "OF"

In math, when you see the word "**of**" between two fractions or a fraction and a number, it's a secret code for one simple operation: **Multiplication!**

"1/2 of 3/4" is the same as "1/2 × 3/4"

Why does "of" mean "times"?

Think of it like this: If you have a chocolate bar (the whole) and you take 1/2 of it, you multiply the whole by 1/2 to find your share. Now, imagine you only have 1/2 of a chocolate bar, and your friend wants 1/2 **of** *that* 1/2. You are finding a **fraction of a fraction**, which requires multiplying the pieces together!

Let's try an example from your notes:

Example: Find 3/5 of 500 meters.

3/5 of 500

= 3/5 × 500

= (3 × 500) / 5

= 3 × 100 (after cancelling 500 and 5)

= 300 meters

Lesson 2: BODMAS - The Traffic Controller!

When an expression has addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division all mixed up, we need a rule to know what to do first. Otherwise, everyone gets a different answer! That rule is **BODMAS** (or PEMDAS in some countries).

B

Brackets

O

Orders (Powers/Roots) & OF

DM

Division / Multiplication (Left to Right)

AS

Addition / Subtraction (Left to Right)

The "O" is Key for Fractions!

For your lesson, remember that the **O (Orders)** includes the "of" operation. Because "of" is just multiplication, this means **multiplication from "of" must be done before regular multiplication (×) or division ($\div$)!**

Example: Simplifying with BODMAS

Simplify: 1/4 + 5/6 × 1/2 $\div$ 4/3 of 3/2

Step 1 (O - Of): Do the "of" first!

4/3 of 3/2 = 4/3 × 3/2 = 2

New Expression: 1/4 + 5/6 × 1/2 $\div$ 2

Step 2 (DM - Left to Right):

First, 5/6 × 1/2 = 5/12

New Expression: 1/4 + 5/12 $\div$ 2

Next, 5/12 $\div$ 2 = 5/12 × 1/2 = 5/24

New Expression: 1/4 + 5/24

Step 3 (AS):

1/4 + 5/24 = 6/24 + 5/24 = 11/24
